DxTF Process Reflection

Dexign the future has been a great experience and a very challenging at the same time. Lot of learning and brainstorming opportunities for me compared to all other courses. Personally it has been a phase to combine all my learnings from other courses taking design from abstract to a vision. I think the course started with our declarative knowledge and perception of the future but gradually moved into a procedural knowledge phase where major emphasis and segmenting was done to embrace the process involved in designing the future scenarios.

The team and its composition was a major factor for maintaining the motivation. The collaboration and solutions provided by each member at critical moments helped to sustain effort over time. We tried to plan the work well in advance, divide it accurately to members who are interested in taking their part. We stuck with each members' strength and skill to get the tasks done. Also async collaboration played a major part and this is where we strategically used the online tools to collaborate. We used various online tools to plan and strategize as well as implement our project. Google Docs, mural.ly , Invisionapp and dropbox share were the tools we relied on for our collaboration.

Scaffolding of the whole course into milestones with explicit deliverables enabled me to focus on the right things, not overshoot requirements or get overwhelmed by the end product being asked. Monitoring performance was difficult but weekly separate team discussion with Arnold and Peter helped tremendously to gauge the progress and expectation. I think as per following the process we team pedagogy felt very encouraged by the response from the faculty advices and also utilized all the strategies recommended from creating benchmarks to developing persona scenarios. I think some battles which I faced was answering the most important but fundamental question to myself again and again "what learning means to a human? ", getting to an answer was not the goal but understanding the question was.

I felt very pleased and motivated when the team decided to work on Learning in 2050. It was in sync with my focus on Learning Science and also very much a challenging project to design for. The intense discussion as a whole class was very useful to get different perspective and input. Looking at other projects made me personally think of ways we could implement our own or measure our performance and strategies against them. I would also like to mention the Field Trips to CreateLab was pleasing and thereafter the expeditionary Treks were really helpful as a learning experience.

One of the stronghold of this course for me has always been Arnold's influence and guidance. The vast experience and knowledge helped and kept us motivated in each step of this course, specially the reflections from Arnold on his blog and dxtf site. Writing our own reflections was a great way for me to express my ideas, observations and thoughts. Its not something I did in the past, but now I think I will be carrying this habit forward with more reflections and observations of designs I encounter in the future. Reading peer reflections on their blogs was truly grasping, a great way to get more in less time.

I think my future path will be so influenced by this course and the knowledge I have grasped as well as the team collaboration and process applied. I hope in the distant future I would definitely reflect back on this Fall 2013 and smile on the scenarios designed which came true.

3D Printing 2500sq Foot House in 20 Hours

This is a new fantastic advancement in 3D printing where one can now print an entire 2500sqft house in 20 Hours and many other complex structures efficiently. It is done via a process called CAD/CAM (computer-aided design / computer-aided manufacturing). 3D models are designed on a computer and then manufactured using CNC Machines or 3D printers. It is manufactured into a physical object automatically, with instruction from 3D computer model to physical object without human interface. The size of the 3D printer is large enough to construct walls by depositing concrete based material layer upon layer to build a wall. This has really fascinated me with the possibilities it portrays for the future of manufacturing.

Phonebloks: Phone worth keeping

A phone only lasts a couple of years before it breaks or becomes obsolete. Although it's often just one part which killed it, we throw everything away since it's almost impossible to repair or upgrade.

So Phonebloks solves this problem as it is made of detachable bloks. The bloks are connected to the base which locks everything together into a solid phone. If a blok breaks you can easily replace it, if it's getting old just upgrade bloks.

Phonebloks solution of blocks based components makes it sustainable and more usable product. This concept can be used to any other forms of devices not only to phones. In this solution there is a good case study on the insight where currently 70% of the waste electronics are in working condition and one component or a part is malfunctioning. The manufacturers also either encourage to upgrade the product and not replace hence the design doesnt allow block level upgrades of electronic goods. It also explores laterally (farsight) and tries to get inspirtation from nature on how it handles misfits or disfunctioning parts. The root cause of the problem is cured by replacing or repairing that particular part and the rest of the system remain intact and efficient.

The Phoneblok project understands that people want this change and they are moving towrds making this concept a reality this is where I see the foresight view of the project. In future, mobile being such a critical aspect of human life currently, this solution will trickle down to other domains and create more sustainable ideas.

While talking about epistemology the context for this project is at the core fundamental principles running the planet. More focus on marketing and sale strategy is overlooking solutions which can be really innovate and sustainable. The impact something like this could be both creative customizable, a reform in thinking and give users more power on what exactly they want with their phones and portable devices.
